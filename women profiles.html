Name: Sitima Lillias

Gender: Female

Age: 36

Level of Education: Diploma in education

Profession: Teacher

Current Occupation: Business

Status: Single mother

Role in the women Group: Secretary

PERSONAL STORY

Lillias is a South Sudanese national residing in Hai TTC in Yei. She was born in Yei on the October 9th, 1988 during the South Sudan liberation war to Mr. Asu William and Mrs. Santina Kwaje Kamba.

Her father died in 2005 and the mother died in 2022.

Lillias got married to Benjamin Landi in 2016 and God has gifted them with four children by names of Mark, Sadrack, Esther and Gideon.

In 2022, the husband Mr Benjamin went to Uganda and ended any form of communication with the wife and children leaving all the responsibility of the four children to Mrs. Lillias.

Currently Lillias operates a very small business of mixed goods in the streets of Hai Nyakama in Yei Town to ensure that her four children go to school, feed and other basic necessities like medication.

According to her, the responsibility is getting heavier since the children are growing and the demand or needs are increasing and in the nearby future things may even fail since her first born child Sadrack is now in primary seven soon joining secondary which requires a lot of financial support.
Lillias is a teacher by profession but due to the poor payment or low salaries of teachers in the schools she decided to focus on the small business she is operating since the money she gets from the teacher can’t cater for the needs of her children.

She’s currently one of the members of our women recycling group.

Name: Florence Gaba Felix

Gender: Female

Age: 53

Level of Education: Senior Two

Current Occupation: cleaner at the county agriculture department

Status: widow

Role in the women Group: Member

PERSONAL STORY

Florence is a South Sudanese national Born in Juba, Kator residential Area on September 15th, 1971 to Mr Poru Felix and Mrs Reida Jabe. Her parents gave birth to 7 seven children of which three died and currently four are alive.

She finished intermediate and joined secondary in 1989 and immediately disrupted by the SPLA liberation war. However, currently she has joined school in Kinji Seconsary under a program called ASEP. It’s a program that caters for the school drop outs who wishes to continue. But due to few teachers in her school, they have stopped the program.

Her parents are now old and the father is now blind while the mother is very sick.

Florence got married in 1990 to Mr Joseph Diko. They gave birth to 7 children before the husband died in 2010.

Three of her children dropped out of school due lack of financial support to cater for fees and other basic necessities since their father died.

Currently she has two of her children in school one in senior four and the other in senior one. She is the one that is struggling to help their education and feeding including other basic needs like soap and medication.

As a government employee in the county agriculture department, they have 13 months without payment. Therefore, everything has turned out to be very hard.

Florence bakes local cookies to sell so that she can raise money for the children school fees. Despite being very sick.

She’s currently one of the members of our women recycling group.
